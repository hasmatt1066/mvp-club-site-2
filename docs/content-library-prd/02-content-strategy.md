## Keyword Opportunity Analysis

### High-Opportunity Keywords (Strong Demand, Moderate Competition)

| Keyword Theme | Why It's an Opportunity | MVP Club Angle |
|---|---|---|
| "AI for [role]" (PM, HR, marketing) | PMI, Atlassian, Coursera investing. Strong intent. | Practice-based, not course enrollment |
| "how to use AI at work" | Broad, high volume. Most results generic. | Workflow-oriented, from coaching experience |
| "ChatGPT vs Claude" | Extremely high search volume. | Honest comparison from daily users |
| "vibe coding" | Growing term, low competition | MVP Club owns this for non-technical audiences |
| "AI for non-technical professionals" | Room for practical, non-academic content | Entire thesis built for this audience |
| "AI adoption" + "why it fails" | HBR, PwC, McKinsey publishing | Coaching-level vs consultant-level insights |
| "AI skills for career advancement" | Career-anxiety searches growing | Career positioning, not certification |

### Lower-Competition, High-Intent (Long Tail)

- "how to use AI for project status reports"
- "AI for performance review writing"
- "how to pitch AI to my boss"
- "AI daily practice routine for professionals"
- "how to build tools with AI no coding"
- "best AI prompt for [specific task]"
- "how to run an AI workshop for my team"

### Keywords to Avoid

- "AI tutorial" (developer-oriented)
- "machine learning course" (technical audience)
- "best AI models" (researcher audience)
- "AI certification" (conflicts with brand)
- "prompt engineering" as standalone (too technical, commoditized)

## Content Differentiation Strategy

### Five Differentiators

**A. "From the Practice" Sections** — Every article includes at least one concrete insight from coaching sessions or community interactions. Short callout box with real observations.

**B. "What Our Members Are Building" Callouts** — Real community member projects, tools, and wins as evidence, not testimonials.

**C. The Career Positioning Lens** — Every article answers: "How does this help me professionally?"

**D. Anti-Slop Editorial Standards** — No em dashes, no cloying transitions, no "wise observer," no invented statistics, simple vocabulary.

**E. Opinionated Takes** — Clear opinions rather than hedging. "We think Claude is better for professional writing than ChatGPT right now, and here's why."

## Article Templates

### Template A: "How to Use AI for [Specific Task]" (How-To Guide)

1. **Hook** (2-3 sentences): Name the task and why it matters
2. **The approach** (1 paragraph): Mental model for this task with AI
3. **Step-by-step walkthrough** (3-6 steps): Concrete instructions with example prompts
4. **Common mistakes** (2-3 bullets): What doesn't work and why
5. **From the Practice** (callout box): Coaching/community insight
6. **Try it yourself** (CTA): Specific next action + community invitation

**Word count:** 1,200-1,800 words

### Template B: "AI for [Role]" (Role-Specific Deep Dive)

1. **Opening**: Acknowledge the reader's reality
2. **Three highest-impact use cases** with examples
3. **Detailed walkthrough of Use Case #1** (quickest win)
4. **Tool recommendations** (1-2 tools, opinionated)
5. **Community member quotes** from people in that role
6. **Career angle**: How AI capability positions you
7. **First week plan** (5-day micro-practice schedule)
8. **FAQ section** (3-4 questions, structured data markup)

**Word count:** 2,000-3,000 words

### Template C: "Career Strategy Piece" (Thought Leadership)

1. **Observation or tension** (stated directly)
2. **The real dynamic at play** (grounded in coaching experience)
3. **What this means for your career** (specific implications)
4. **What to do about it** (concrete actions)
5. **The invitation** (community mention, not hard sell)

**Word count:** 1,500-2,500 words

### Template D: "Tool Breakdown" (Comparison/Review)

1. **What we're comparing and why**
2. **Quick verdict** (upfront, don't bury it)
3. **Comparison table** (by use case, not just features)
4. **Detailed breakdown by use case**
5. **What we use at MVP Club and why**
6. **Community preferences**
7. **The bottom line**
8. **Update log** (date of last review)

**Word count:** 1,500-2,000 words (update quarterly)

## Internal Linking Strategy

### Hub-and-Spoke Architecture

```
                    RESOURCE LIBRARY HOME
                    (index of all pillars)
                           |
        +------+------+----+----+------+------+
        v      v      v         v      v      v
    Pillar   Pillar  Pillar  Pillar  Pillar  Pillar
    Page 1   Page 2  Page 3  Page 4  Page 5  Page 6
       |        |       |       |       |       |
    8-12     8-12    8-12    8-12    8-12    8-12
    cluster  cluster cluster cluster cluster cluster
```

### Linking Rules

1. Every cluster article links UP to its pillar page
2. Every pillar page links DOWN to all its cluster articles
3. Cross-pillar links where natural
4. Every article links to at least 2 other internal articles
5. Pillar pages link to community signup with mid-page and end-of-page CTAs

### CTA Strategy by Article Type

| Article Type | CTA Style |
|---|---|
| How-to guides | Soft: "Want to practice this with other professionals?" |
| Role-specific guides | Medium: "Members in [role] share workflows weekly." |
| Career strategy | Contextual: Natural invitation in the conclusion |
| Tool breakdowns | Soft: "Our community debates tools constantly." |
