# MVP Club Brand & Editorial Guide

This is the single source of truth for brand voice, editorial standards, and founder voice profiles when creating content for mvpclub.ai. Reference this document before writing any article, blog post, or learn resource.

---

## Core Thesis

**AI adoption is a practiced skill.** The barriers are not technical — they're emotional, motivational, and time-based.

**Human + AI > Human OR AI.** An AI-augmented workforce will outperform an AI-replacement workforce.

**Practice > Training.** Daily use builds capability. Workshops don't. Understanding comes through doing, not preparation.

**Better together than alone.** AI is going to disrupt all of our professional careers, and it is better to go through that disruption together rather than alone.

---

## What We Are / What We Are Not

**We are:**
- A coaching company (not a training provider)
- Peer experts who guide professionals through transformation
- Community builders creating space for practice and support

**We are NOT:**
- A training provider (we don't build courses)
- Content-focused "sage on the stage"
- Technical gatekeepers
- Point-in-time intervention

## Writing Style Rules

### Always Do
1. **Lead with the human**, not the technology
2. **Make it actionable** — what can they do today?
3. **Be specific** — real examples, specific tools, dollar amounts, time frames
4. **Invite, don't lecture** — we're peers, not professors
5. **Name specific tools** (Claude, ChatGPT, Cline), never just "AI"
6. **Use simple vocabulary** — fun, wins, getting better
7. **Action first, then feeling** — ground in what you DO, then name how it FEELS
8. **Start mid-thought** — no wind-up intros, no throat-clearing preambles

### Never Do
1. **Exploit fear** — don't scare people into action
2. **Overpromise speed** — this is a long journey
3. **Use jargon to impress** — accessibility over authority
4. **Focus on tools over people** — the human is the story
5. **Promise automation** — we're about augmentation
6. **Fabricate examples** — never invent statistics, anecdotes, or experiences. If you need a real example, ask the founder or leave a [NEEDS EXAMPLE] placeholder.

### Sentence-Level Rules
- No em dashes — use commas, periods, colons, or parentheses instead
- No "wise observer" setups or humble-brags
- No cloying transitions ("Here's what most people don't realize")
- No wind-up intros ("Something I keep coming back to:", "I've been thinking about:")
- First sentence of every article hooks with a specific detail, not a generality
- Paragraphs max 4 sentences for web readability
- Vary sentence length: short punchy sentences after longer ones
- Use "you" more than "we" in instructional content

### Structure-Level Rules
- Every article needs at least one real community member quote or story
- Every article needs at least one "I tried this and here's what happened" moment
- Subheadings should be readable as a standalone outline
- No more than 3 paragraphs before the first subheading
- CTA is an invitation, not a sales pitch

## Prohibited Language

| Term | Why |
|---|---|
| Upskilling / Reskilling | Corporate jargon, implies deficit |
| AI literacy | Sounds like school |
| Future-proof your career | Fear-based marketing |
| Training program / modules | We don't do training |
| AI education | We're not educators |
| Strategic transformation | Consultant-speak |
| Change management | Corporate, not human |
| Company-wide rollout | We start small |
| AI mastery | Overpromising |
| Unlock your potential | Generic self-help |

### LinkedIn Formula Patterns to Avoid
- "I'm going to say something controversial..."
- "Hot take:" / "Unpopular opinion:"
- "Here's what nobody tells you about..."
- "Most people think X. They're wrong."
- Opening with a "bold statement" that's actually a safe take
- Ending with a question purely for engagement
- The humble-brag transformation story
- "Nobody" statements ("Nobody planned it." "Nobody talks about this.")
- "\_\_\_ isn't \_\_\_. It's \_\_\_." reversals (AI writing tell)

## Target Audience

### Primary Persona: The Mid-Career Professional
- White-collar professional, not a developer
- Busy, has a clear job already
- Wants to apply AI to their existing role
- Wants to position themselves as an AI leader on their team
- Sees AI adoption as a career advancement opportunity
- May be skeptical or nervous
- Short on time, wants crisp applicable advice

### What They Feel
- Overwhelmed by the pace of change
- Short on time to learn new tools
- Uncertain about what AI means for their role
- Skeptical of hype and overpromises
- Wanting guidance, not lectures

### What They Need
- Permission to start small
- Confidence that they can do this
- Efficient use of their limited time
- A supportive community
- Practical, relevant guidance

## The MVP Club Test

Before publishing any content, ask:

1. Does it lead with the human, not the tech?
2. Does it invite rather than lecture?
3. Does it acknowledge the journey takes time?
4. Does it avoid fear-based motivation?
5. Would a skeptical, time-strapped professional feel welcomed?

**If yes to all five, it's on-brand.**

